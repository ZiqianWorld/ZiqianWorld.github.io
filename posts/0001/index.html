I was wondering whether the neural activities in the human brain (e.g., neural functions) that correspond to a specific psychological behaviour (e.g., decision making) are one-to-one correspondences between individuals, or just appear some kind of similar in specific brain areas? I doubt it’s the latter. For example, someone may train a Bert, while others train a GPT.

Phonological awareness is also useful during spelling, this is what I learned from personal experience. Taking “engieering” as an example of long English words (well, at least, for English beginning learners, it’s long), every time I write “engieering”, I will first think about “engine”, and then realise that the second half should be something like “deer” puls “ing”. Which means, the spelling of long words can be decoded as the combination of some short words and/or compounds. Such a process should become automated and unconscious after practising.

Chinese people can “remedy” the lack of an element in 五行 (i.e., gold, wood, water, fire, earth) by naming themselves with a special Chinese character. For example, if you lack wood (木), your parents propably would add 森 or 林 in your name (many woods here, right?). But I was wondering, how do foreigners remedy this? They don’t have Chiense characters (logographic language) in their names! Turns out, such a process is just an unscientific Chinese traditional belief to help lazyman name their children without effort.

I was reading a New York Times article, which introduces stuff about a pre-eminent professor. So, I just realised that the name of the rapper Eminem implies eminent!? How interesting. I really need to read more to be a pro.

9/25 Love in Education
#

I want to cultivate professional, passionate students who know what love is and can love others. Love is crucial and has been neglected in education. We should love students equally, and teach them using professional knowledge, rather than persuade /pəˈsweɪd/ them using the unfair status and fake reasons (you know, students may be not as professional as you in your expert domain, but they are not stupid). We should love them equally. Of course, I don’t mean respect is unnecessary. Adults keep talking with ridicule that our generation “grew up in a sealed jar”. I think it’s not a flaw, it’s an advantage: only those who have been loved know how to love others and treat others kindly.

I am tring a “specific citaiton” method. That means, I will try to mention the specific page(s) when quote others’ opinions/statements. Instead, when mentioning some general conclusions from studies, I will keep the general APA citation format. I think many reserchers (especially students) do not dinstinguish “quoting” and “citing” since these two distinct concepts share the same word in Chinese (i.e., 引用）. Maybe I should emphasise that in my future class (if I have) and encourage using this precise citation method.
