11/13 Phylosophy
#

Waiting at a bus stop and consulting Hegel’s philosophy (黑格尔的哲学) with DeepSeek, I surprisingly found that Hegel’s dialectics (thesis, antithesis, and synthesis) are consistent with the writing of a Fugue (i.e., subject, answer, and countersubject). An advantage of AI is that I can fulfil my curiosity anywhere by exploring things I’d like to know, other than fast-food information on social media. This time, I learned that one’s philosophy can be summarised by concepts including:

  • First Principle: This is where every other knowledge is inferred and derived from.
  • Metaphysics: how interesting. In Chinese, it’s called 形而上学, but I found that you can better understand its meaning through the English name. In brief, metaphysics is the knowledge other than physics.
  • Epistemology: it’s about what knowledge is and where it comes from. In reading comprehension research, epistemological knowledge is also a significant predictor of reading success.
  • and Ethics: I am less interested in this one.

Training AI models (the integration of human knowledge) nowadays seems similar to unveiling the Absolute Spirit, which is a key concept of the First Principle in Hegel’s philosophy, and also similar to what people did in The Last Question, an Isaac Asimov science fiction short story, and The Nine Billion Names of God, an Arthur Clarke science fiction short story.

Therefore, philosophers always need to begin by establishing a First Principle. A well-known example is “I think, therefore I am”, by Descartes (笛卡尔). It seems reasonable, right? You consider your own thinking as the only thing you really trust, and then infer every other knowledge from this. Could we push the truth further? Yes, there must be something before the existence of your mind! For instance, the existence itself! Here we find Existentialism (存在主义). From this perspective, either Descartes or the existentialists seems to prefer idealism (唯心主义). At the same time, Marx appears to be on the contrary side, that is, materialism (唯物主义), as he believes that material conditions determine society. However, when I wrote this, DeepSeek reminded me that Marx is a firm materialist. Still, Descartes agrees with both idealism and materialism (as he agrees with the existence of the world, unlike Berkeley, who believes that “to be is to be perceived”). Many existentialists think beyond the traditional binary taxonomy. You see, philosophy is fun.

11/18 Hyperion
#

Hyperion, a science fiction, considers humans’ love as a very great thing, even on the universe level. I don’t believe that. Animals have love, too, though they express it in simpler ways, and love is only one of the mental activities we share. We have a prefrontal cortex that enables us to perform complex forms of love. What if aliens have a more complex brain than humans’? What if they consider us as lab mice? On Earth, we do not consider a lab mouse’s psychological activities as the greatest thing of all. This cannot save the universe. Despite that, I still consider love to be great, but only for individuals. The truth is, love may be insignificant and unimportant within the scope of the universe, so do humans, but love matters to us.
